Shodan

Discription: This is a search engine developed by John Matherly. Almost all IP address can be searched, making this a source to find unprotected webcams, SCADA/ICS sites, and other IoT devices. Network flow events, proxy logs and firewall logs can be retrieved

Justifications: With online learning, a lot of students’ personal devices are used at home with computers and webcams exposed. Shodan will be able to reveal a lot of vulnerable IPs which in turn inform the protective strategy that can be taken by the education institutions.

Expoit-DB

Description: Often referred as ‘Google Dorks’, Google Hacking Database (GHD) contains custom Google search terms to expose sensitive information including usernames, passwords and vulnerable servers.

Justifications: Children information are often exposed without awareness even after months. The awareness of cybersecurity in education is in general very low. Hence, the schools can use this GHD to verify if the student information has been exposed with custom search strings.

SploitUs

Description: Sploitus is a search engine for publicly available exploits of vulnerable software. If the schools are using a specific software with exploits, the schools can upgrade and patch in time.

Justifications: Another main reason of K12 schools being targeted are the lack of cybersecurity department and hence delays of upgrading software and usage of compromised software. Sploitus will pinpoint vulnerabilities in these situations.

Haveibeenpwned

Description: This site checks whether email and social accounts have been compromised. This can be searched by school domain name as well, providing assessment of the risk of hackers pretending to be legit stakeholder and send phishing emails

Justifications: Stolen accounts are often the first step of launching subsequent attacks. Ransomware starts from sending phishing email from stolen email access, while class invasion can be done by gaining access to Zoom with compromised registered email account.
