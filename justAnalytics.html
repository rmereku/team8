Reputation Analysis

Why do we do this? What is the data source? Why do we pick this over other analytics approaches? What will we do?
Reputation analysis will help us identify compromised or suspicious devices and IPs.

This will help us block access with malicious intent to virtual classes, student records and accounting systems. 		Shodan:
  • IP address of unprotected devices
  • Domain names
  • File hashes
HaveIbeenpwned:
  • Compromised email & social accounts
  • Domain names
  • Identity theft is often the starting point of cybersecurity incidents in K-12 schools.
  • Student accounts have been used to intrude virtual classes to show pornography or hate speech.
  • Phishing emails are often started from compromised digital identities which then capture sensitive financial information from other legit administrators.
  • Reputation analysis will help block the vulnerability and act at the root cause.
  • Identifies suspicious domain names, IP addresses and file hashes
  • Highlights high risk communications such as malware intended to communicate with known contaminated IPs or domain

Anomaly Detection

Why do we do this? What is the data source? Why do we pick this over other analytics approaches? What will we do?
Anomaly detection identifies activities that are different from normal activities.

Common threats of ransomware, phishing and class invasions can all leave traits of anomaly activities that can be traced. 		Internal Network Data
  • Network flow events
  • Full packet capture
  • DNS data
  • DHCP logs
  • e-mail metadata
Perimeter network data
  • network flow events
  • proxy logs
  • firewall logs
  • full packet capture
  • At K-12 schools, students, teachers and parents’ activities are regular with repetitive schedules.
  • Anomaly is a strong sign of cybersecurity threats.
Conducting time-series anomaly detection
  • Predictive confidence level approach : Use historical data to build a predictive model to identify overall common trend and patterns to benchmark against anomalies
  • Statistical profiling approach: Use historical data’s standard deviation to define the band that defines normal value and anything otherwise an anomaly
  • Clustering based unsupervised approach: Dynamically estimate the number of clusters without prior labeling.

Malware Analysis

Why do we do this? What is the data source? Why do we pick this over other analytics approaches? What will we do?
Malware is the key risks identified that students and staff are often tricked into downloading malware to use at schools. The data source is the credibility ratings given by a community of trustworthy cybersecurity companies and portals.
  • Students and staff are often tricked by phishing email and malicious sites into installing malwares, which then opened the door to subsequent attacks such as virtual class intrusion and financial data compromise.
  • Hence, malware analysis is key to block these subsequent threats.
 Conducting time-series anomaly detection
  • We will enter the digital identity of the school on virustotal.com
  • Virustotal.com will then analyzes the credibility ratings given by the community of trustworthy cybersecurity companies and portals.
